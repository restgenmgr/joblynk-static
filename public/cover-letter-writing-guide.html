How to Write a Cover Letter That Gets Noticed

Most cover letters are ignored. Here is how to write one that makes a recruiter pick up the phone.

📅 June 2026✍️ JobLynk Editorial⏱ 8 min read
Cover LetterJob Application

A great cover letter can get you an interview even when your resume is not perfect. In India's competitive market, cover letter writing remains one of the most underused advantages available to job seekers.

1. Why Cover Letters Still Matter

A 2025 LinkedIn survey found 72% of hiring managers read cover letters when provided, and over half say a compelling letter influences shortlisting decisions. In management, creative, and client-facing roles, a cover letter is often the deciding factor between two equally qualified candidates.

2. The Four-Paragraph Structure That Works

  • Paragraph 1 — The Hook: Open with a specific achievement or compelling reason you want this exact role at this company. Never start with "I am applying for..."
  • Paragraph 2 — Your Fit: Connect your most relevant experience to the top 2–3 requirements in the job description. Use numbers.
  • Paragraph 3 — Why Them: Mention something specific about the company — recent initiative, culture, product — and connect it to why you want to work there.
  • Paragraph 4 — The Close: Confidently ask for the next step.
✅ STRONG OPENING
"When Zomato's hyperlocal campaign drove a 400% spike in weekend orders across Tier-2 cities last year, it confirmed what I have spent 5 years building toward: performance marketing that moves real numbers in the Indian consumer market. That campaign is exactly why I am applying to lead your growth marketing team."
💡 The Golden RuleNever start with "I". Start with the company, the role, or an achievement. It signals you are focused on them, not yourself.

3. Tailor Every Letter

Recruiters can tell instantly when a letter is generic. At minimum, customise the company name, role title, hook paragraph, and "why them" paragraph for every application.

4. Keep It to One Page

250–350 words is ideal. Cut every sentence that does not add direct value. A tight letter that respects the reader's time signals professional maturity.

5. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Starting with "To Whom It May Concern" — find the hiring manager's name on LinkedIn
  • Repeating your entire resume — the letter should complement, not duplicate
  • Using phrases like "passionate team player" or "detail-oriented"
  • Forgetting to proofread — one typo can end your candidacy

Conclusion

Mastering cover letter writing is one of the highest-ROI skills in your job search toolkit. Start with the structure above, customise for each application, and browse verified openings on JobLynk to put it to work.

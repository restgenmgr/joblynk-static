Salary Negotiation Tips to Get Paid What You Deserve

Most candidates accept the first offer. Here is how to negotiate confidently and walk away with significantly more.

📅 June 2025✍️ JobLynk Editorial⏱ 7 min read

SalaryNegotiation

Studies consistently show that fewer than 40% of Indian job seekers negotiate their salary offer — yet those who do earn an average of 7–15% more than those who accept the first number. Over a career, that gap compounds into lakhs of rupees. These salary negotiation tips will give you the exact words, the right timing, and the confidence to ask for what you are worth.

1. Research Before You Walk In

Never negotiate blind. Before any salary conversation, arm yourself with market data from AmbitionBox, Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary Insights, and Naukri's salary tool. Know the range for your role, experience level, city, and company size. When you cite a number, cite it with evidence — not just hope.

  • Search: "[Your role] salary [city] [years experience] India 2025"
  • Check 3–5 sources and find the median, not just the highest
  • Factor in company size — an MNC pays 20–40% more than an Indian SME for equivalent roles

2. Never Give a Number First

When asked "What are your salary expectations?" early in the process, deflect gracefully. Giving a number too early anchors the negotiation against you.

💬 What to say
"I would love to understand the full scope of the role and the overall compensation structure before discussing a specific number. Could you share the budgeted range for this position?"

If they press you for a number, give a range with your target at the bottom — not the middle. If you want ₹12 LPA, say "₹12–14 LPA based on my research and the responsibilities involved."

3. Let the Offer Land — Then Pause

When an offer comes in, do not respond immediately. Even if it is great, pause. Say "Thank you — I am genuinely excited about this role. Can I have 24–48 hours to review the complete offer?" This is professional, expected, and buys you time to plan your counter.

💡 The Silence TrickAfter you state your counter-number, go completely silent. The next person to speak loses leverage. Most Indian candidates fill the silence nervously — do not. Let them respond.

4. The Counter-Offer Formula

A strong counter-offer has three parts: appreciation, anchor, and justification.

💬 Counter-offer script
"Thank you for the offer — I am very excited about joining [Company] and I am confident I can make a strong contribution. Based on my research into market rates for this role in [city], and considering my [X years of experience / specific skill / achievement], I was expecting something closer to ₹[your number]. Is there flexibility to get to that range?"

Notice: you are not demanding, you are asking. You are providing justification. And you are leaving the door open for them to move.

5. Negotiate the Full Package — Not Just Base Salary

If they cannot move on base salary, negotiate everything else:

  • Performance bonus percentage and review cycle
  • Joining bonus (especially if you are forfeiting unvested ESOPs or a bonus at your current job)
  • Remote work flexibility
  • Extra leave days or flexible hours
  • Professional development budget (courses, conferences)
  • Early performance review (3 months instead of 12)

Each of these has real monetary value. A ₹50,000 training budget, 5 extra leave days, and a 3-month early review can be worth ₹1.5–2 LPA in real terms.

6. Know Your Walk-Away Number

Before negotiating, decide your minimum acceptable offer. This is your BATNA — Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement. If you have another offer, a current job, or strong market demand, your BATNA is high and you negotiate from strength. If you are unemployed with no alternatives, your BATNA is weak — be realistic.

⚠️ Do Not BluffNever claim you have another offer unless you actually do. If they call your bluff and ask for proof, or if they let you walk, the relationship is damaged before it starts.

7. Get It in Writing

Once agreed, get every element of the offer in writing before you resign from your current job. Verbal agreements mean nothing in a dispute. The offer letter should specify: base salary, bonus structure, designation, start date, and any special terms negotiated.

Conclusion

These salary negotiation tips work because they are grounded in research, delivered with confidence, and backed by legitimate justification. Negotiating is not aggressive — it is professional.

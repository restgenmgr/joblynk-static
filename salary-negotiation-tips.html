Salary Negotiation Tips to Get Paid What You Deserve

Most candidates accept the first offer. Here is how to negotiate confidently and walk away with significantly more.

📅 June 2026✍️ JobLynk Editorial⏱ 8 min read
SalaryNegotiation

Fewer than 40% of Indian job seekers negotiate their salary — yet those who do earn 7–15% more on average. Over a career, that gap compounds into lakhs. These salary negotiation tips give you the exact words, timing, and confidence to ask for what you are worth.

1. Research Before You Walk In

Never negotiate blind. Use AmbitionBox, Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary Insights, and Naukri's salary tool. Know the median range for your role, experience level, city, and company size. An MNC pays 20–40% more than an Indian SME for equivalent roles.

2. Never Give a Number First

💬 WHAT TO SAY
"I would love to understand the full scope of the role before discussing a specific number. Could you share the budgeted range for this position?"

If pressed, give a range with your target at the bottom: if you want ₹12 LPA, say "₹12–14 LPA based on my research and the responsibilities involved."

3. Let the Offer Land — Then Pause

When an offer comes in, say: "Thank you — I am genuinely excited about this role. Can I have 24–48 hours to review the complete offer?" This is professional and buys you time to plan your counter.

💡 The Silence TrickAfter you state your counter-number, go completely silent. The next person to speak loses leverage. Most candidates fill the silence nervously — do not.

4. The Counter-Offer Script

💬 COUNTER-OFFER
"Thank you for the offer — I am very excited about joining [Company]. Based on my research into market rates for this role in [city], and considering my [X years / specific skill / achievement], I was expecting something closer to ₹[your number]. Is there flexibility to get to that range?"

5. Negotiate the Full Package

If they cannot move on base salary, negotiate: performance bonus, joining bonus (especially if forfeiting unvested ESOPs), remote work flexibility, extra leave days, professional development budget, and early performance review (3 months instead of 12).

⚠️ Do Not BluffNever claim you have another offer unless you actually do. If they call your bluff, the relationship is damaged before it starts.

6. Get It in Writing

Get every element of the offer in writing before you resign from your current job. The offer letter should specify base salary, bonus structure, designation, start date, and any special terms negotiated.

How to Write a Cover Letter That Gets Noticed

Most cover letters are ignored. Here is how to write one that makes a recruiter stop scrolling and pick up the phone.

📅 June 2025✍️ JobLynk Editorial⏱ 7 min read

Cover LetterJob Application

A great cover letter can get you an interview even when your resume is not perfect. A bad one — or no letter at all — can cost you a role you were perfectly qualified for. In India's competitive job market, cover letter writing remains one of the most underused advantages available to job seekers. This guide shows you exactly how to write one that commands attention.

1. Why Cover Letters Still Matter in 2025

Many candidates assume cover letters are obsolete. They are wrong. A 2024 LinkedIn survey found that 72% of hiring managers read cover letters when provided, and over half say a compelling letter influences shortlisting decisions. In competitive roles — management, creative fields, client-facing positions — a cover letter is often the deciding factor between two equally qualified candidates.

2. The Structure That Works

Effective cover letter writing follows a clear four-paragraph structure:

  • Paragraph 1 — The Hook: Open with something that grabs attention. Not "I am applying for the role of..." — everyone starts that way. Instead, lead with a specific achievement or a compelling reason you want this exact role at this exact company.
  • Paragraph 2 — Your Fit: Connect your most relevant experience to the top 2–3 requirements in the job description. Use specific examples and numbers.
  • Paragraph 3 — Why Them: Show you have done your research. Mention something specific about the company — a recent initiative, their culture, a product you admire — and connect it to why you want to work there.
  • Paragraph 4 — The Close: Confidently ask for the next step. "I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how I can contribute to [Company's] goals. I am available for a call at your convenience."

3. A Real Example: Before and After

❌ Weak Opening
"I am writing to apply for the Marketing Manager position advertised on your website. I have 5 years of experience in marketing and believe I would be a great fit for your team."
✅ Strong Opening
"When Zomato's hyperlocal campaign drove a 400% spike in weekend orders across Tier-2 cities last year, it confirmed what I have spent 5 years building toward: performance marketing that moves real numbers in the Indian consumer market. That campaign is exactly why I am applying to lead your growth marketing team."
💡 The Golden RuleNever start with "I". Start with the company, the role, or an achievement. It immediately signals you are focused on them, not yourself.

4. Tailor Every Single Letter

The biggest cover letter mistake is sending the same letter to every company. Recruiters can tell instantly. At minimum, customise: the company name, the specific role title, the hook paragraph, and the "why them" paragraph. The middle section can be adapted from a template, but the beginning and end must be fresh each time.

5. Keep It to One Page

Your cover letter should never exceed one page — ideally 250–350 words. Hiring managers are busy. A tight, punchy letter that respects their time signals professional maturity. Cut every sentence that does not add direct value.

Pair your cover letter with a strong resume. Read our complete guide on how to write a winning resume for the full application package. And once you have both ready, make sure your LinkedIn profile tells the same story consistently.

6. Common Cover Letter Mistakes to Avoid

  • Starting with "To Whom It May Concern" — find the hiring manager's name on LinkedIn
  • Repeating your entire resume — the letter should complement, not duplicate
  • Using generic phrases like "passionate team player" or "detail-oriented"
  • Forgetting to proofread — one typo can end your candidacy
  • Writing in a stiff, formal tone — professional but human always wins

Conclusion

Mastering cover letter writing is one of the highest-ROI skills in your job search toolkit. It takes extra time, but the candidates who invest in it consistently outperform those who do not.

How to Rank #1 on Google in 2025: The Ultimate Dos & Don'ts Guide

📅 April 29, 2025 | ⏱ 12 min read | 🏷️ SEO, Digital Marketing, Content Strategy

The brutal truth about SEO: Over 90% of web pages get zero traffic from Google. The gap between page one and page two isn't a small step — it's a cliff. If your website isn't on the first page, it might as well be invisible. But here's the good news: ranking #1 on Google is not reserved for big brands with massive budgets. It's a skill. And in this guide, we're breaking it all down — the Dos, the Don'ts, and the strategies that actually work in 2025.

Why Ranking #1 on Google Still Matters More Than Ever

The first organic result on Google captures an average click-through rate (CTR) of 27.6%, while the second position drops to around 15%, and by the time you reach position ten, you're looking at barely 2.5%. That's not a linear decline — it's a dramatic drop-off that makes the #1 spot exponentially more valuable than everything below it.

With the rise of AI-powered search features like Google's AI Overviews, featured snippets, and People Also Ask boxes, visibility in 2025 is more nuanced than ever. But the fundamentals of great SEO — relevance, authority, and user experience — remain the bedrock of every top-ranking page.

PART 1: THE DOS — What You Must Do to Rank #1 on Google

✅ DO: Conduct Deep, Intent-Driven Keyword Research

Keyword research is the foundation of every successful SEO strategy. But in 2025, it's not just about search volume — it's about search intent. Google's algorithm understands why someone is searching.

  • Use tools like Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, Semrush, or Ubersuggest.
  • Target long-tail keywords (3–5 word phrases) with lower competition and higher intent.
  • Analyse the SERP for your target keyword to understand what format and depth Google rewards.
  • Identify keyword clusters to build content hubs.
Pro Tip: Use Google's "People Also Ask" and "Related Searches" sections for endless content ideas.

✅ DO: Create Comprehensive, High-Quality Content That Solves Problems

Google's Helpful Content System rewards content created for humans, not search engines. Your content must be comprehensive, original, well-structured, and up-to-date.

If your target keyword is "how to start a podcast," don't write 600 words. Top results often have 2,000 to 4,000 words. Match or exceed that depth.

✅ DO: Master On-Page SEO

ElementBest Practice
Title TagInclude primary keyword near the beginning. Keep under 60 characters.
Meta Description150–160 characters. Include keyword and a compelling CTA.
H1 TagOne per page. Should match or closely reflect the title tag.
URL StructureShort, descriptive, keyword-rich (e.g., /how-to-rank-on-google)
Image Alt TextDescribe every image using relevant keywords naturally.
Internal LinksLink to related content on your site using descriptive anchor text.
Schema MarkupAdd structured data (FAQ, How-To, Article) to enhance SERP appearance.

✅ DO: Build a Strong, Authoritative Backlink Profile

Backlinks remain one of Google's most powerful ranking signals. Focus on quality over quantity. High-value sources: guest posts, digital PR, resource pages, broken link building, and original research.

✅ DO: Prioritise Technical SEO

  • Page Speed: Aim for >90 on Google PageSpeed Insights.
  • Mobile-First Indexing: Ensure full responsiveness.
  • Core Web Vitals: Optimise LCP, INP, and CLS.
  • HTTPS: Always use SSL.
  • Crawlability: Use XML sitemap, fix broken links, eliminate duplicate content.
  • Structured Data: Implement JSON-LD schema.

✅ DO: Optimise for User Experience and Engagement Signals

High bounce rates and low dwell time hurt rankings. Use clean fonts, break up paragraphs, add a table of contents, and reduce intrusive popups.

✅ DO: Build Topical Authority Through Content Clusters

Create pillar pages (comprehensive guides) and cluster pages (subtopics linking back to the pillar). This demonstrates E‑E‑A‑T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness).

PART 2: THE DON'TS — What Will Destroy Your Google Rankings

❌ DON'T: Stuff Keywords Into Your Content

Keyword stuffing is penalised. Write naturally. Use synonyms and related terms.

❌ DON'T: Buy Low-Quality Backlinks

Link farms and PBNs can lead to manual penalties. Earn links through quality content and genuine outreach.

❌ DON'T: Neglect Your Existing Content

Content decays. Refresh older posts with updated statistics, new internal links, and expanded sections.

❌ DON'T: Ignore Local SEO (If You Have a Physical Presence)

Claim your Google Business Profile, ensure NAP consistency, collect reviews, and use local schema.

❌ DON'T: Publish Thin, Low-Value Content

300‑word blog posts won't rank. Every piece should pass the "Is this genuinely helpful?" test.

❌ DON'T: Underestimate the Power of Internal Linking

Use descriptive anchor text, avoid orphan pages, and link from older posts to newer ones.

❌ DON'T: Ignore Google Search Console

Monitor performance, coverage, Core Web Vitals, manual actions, and backlinks.

PART 3: BONUS TIPS — Advanced Strategies to Dominate Google in 2025

  • Optimise for Featured Snippets – Answer questions concisely (40–60 words) under a clear heading.
  • Leverage Video Content – Embed relevant videos, transcribe them, and boost dwell time.
  • Track Your Competitors – Use Ahrefs or Semrush to identify gaps and produce 10x better content.

Quick Reference: Google Ranking Dos & Don'ts Cheat Sheet

  • ✅ DO: Intent-driven keyword research, comprehensive content, on‑page SEO, quality backlinks, technical fixes, Core Web Vitals, content clusters, featured snippets.
  • ❌ DON'T: Keyword stuffing, buying backlinks, neglecting old content, ignoring mobile, thin content, poor internal linking, ignoring Search Console.

Final Thoughts: Ranking #1 on Google Is a Marathon, Not a Sprint

SEO results typically take 3–6 months. Most competitors cut corners. If you commit to doing the work properly — starting with one keyword, creating the best content, optimising technically, and earning quality backlinks — ranking on the first page is absolutely within your reach.
